SECTION 2: ANALYTICAL ABILITY ‚Äî DRAWING CONCLUSIONS

Passing Score: 4/5

Direction: Each item in this section consists of statements followed by several possible conclusions. Determine the best conclusion that can be logically made from the given statements.

1. STATEMENT: He can join the team if he passes the tryouts.

  • A. Passing the tryouts is what allows him to join the team.
  • B. Even if he fails the tryouts, he can still join the team.
  • C. The only way to join the team is to pass the tryouts and nothing else.
  • D. Passing the tryouts guarantees that he will succeed in the team.
  • E. Without passing the tryouts, joining the team is impossible.

2. STATEMENT: Iron fortified salt could reduce anemia.

  • A. A new process enables ordinary table salt to be fortified with iron.
  • B. This advance could help reduce the high incidence of anemia in the world's population due to a deficiency of iron in the diet.
  • C. Salt is used as a preservative for food and a flavor enhancer all over the globe.
  • D. People consume salt in quantities that would provide iron in significant amounts.
  • E. None of the above

3. STATEMENTS: Global temperatures have been rising steadily over the past decades. Deforestation contributes to the rise of global temperatures.

  • A. Reforestation is the only solution to stop global warming.
  • B. Global warming is entirely caused by human activities like deforestation.
  • C. If deforestation continues, global temperatures may keep rising.
  • D. Climate change affects only areas where forests are being cut down.
  • E. The rate of deforestation determines the exact degree of temperature rise.

4. STATEMENT: Studies show that employees who take regular breaks are more productive than those who work continuously without breaks.

  • A. Productivity depends on taking breaks.
  • B. Employees who work without breaks are always unproductive.
  • C. Taking regular breaks can contribute to higher productivity.
  • D. Employees who take longer breaks work better than those who take shorter breaks.
  • E. Productivity and working hours are unrelated.

5. STATEMENT: A recent study found that more commuters use buses than trains in the city.

  • A. No one uses trains anymore in the city.
  • B. Fewer commuters use trains than buses in the city.
  • C. Twice as many commuters use buses compared to trains.
  • D. Almost all commuters in the city prefer buses over trains.
  • E. The number of train commuters is equal to the number of bus commuters.
